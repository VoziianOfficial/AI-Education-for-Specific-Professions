Privacy Overview

Information Is Used for Clear and Limited Purposes

Rolewise AI is an educational information and inquiry platform. The website may receive information when you submit a form, communicate with us, manage cookie choices, or interact with website features. We aim to limit collection to information reasonably necessary for the relevant purpose.

This policy is a general explanation of website privacy practices. Specific processing may vary depending on the nature of an inquiry, the services used to operate the website, applicable law, and any additional notice presented at the point of collection.

Scope and Purpose

This Privacy Policy applies to information processed in connection with the Rolewise AI website, including the public pages, contact form, cookie preference tools, educational content, advertising and collaboration inquiries, and related communications.

It does not automatically apply to third-party websites, course providers, trainers, software tools, advertisers, collaborators, external platforms, or other independent organizations that may be mentioned, linked, introduced, or discussed through Rolewise AI.

Independent third parties

External organizations may operate under their own privacy policies, terms, security controls, retention periods, and legal responsibilities. Review their policies before sharing information with them.

Information We May Collect

The information collected depends on how you use the website and what you choose to submit.

Inquiry information Name, email address, optional phone number, inquiry category, message, source page, consent confirmation, and other information voluntarily included in a form submission.
Communication information Information contained in emails, replies, follow-up messages, collaboration discussions, advertising inquiries, correction requests, accessibility requests, or other direct communications.
Technical information Internet Protocol address, browser type, device type, operating system, referring page, requested page, timestamps, language settings, approximate region, and diagnostic or security-related information.
Preference information Cookie consent choices, interface preferences, accessibility preferences, and other settings stored to remember your selections.
Usage information Information about pages viewed, navigation paths, interactions, referring sources, approximate session duration, and general website performance where permitted and enabled.

Information we do not request through the general inquiry form

  • Passwords, authentication codes, security answers, or account credentials.
  • Payment card details, bank account information, or cryptocurrency wallet credentials.
  • Government identification documents or national identification numbers.
  • Health records, medical information, or highly sensitive personal information.
  • Confidential client, employee, customer, student, patient, or transaction records.
  • Proprietary datasets, source code, trade secrets, or restricted business documents.

How Information May Be Collected

Information may be collected through several routes:

  • Directly from you when you submit a contact form or send a message.
  • Through follow-up communication connected with an inquiry.
  • Automatically through server logs, security systems, browser storage, cookies, or similar technologies.
  • From service providers that support hosting, email delivery, website security, analytics, form processing, or technical operations.
  • From publicly available professional or organizational information where relevant to a submitted collaboration or business inquiry.

You are responsible for ensuring that you have authority to submit information about another person, organization, client, employee, or business contact.

How Information May Be Used

Information may be used to:

  • Receive, review, organize, and respond to inquiries.
  • Understand profession-specific learning, workshop, course, team training, advertising, or collaboration interests.
  • Request clarification or additional non-sensitive context.
  • Provide educational information or explain a possible inquiry route.
  • Evaluate the relevance and suitability of advertising, provider, resource, event, or collaboration submissions.
  • Operate, maintain, secure, troubleshoot, and improve the website.
  • Remember cookie preferences and other website settings.
  • Detect misuse, spam, abuse, unauthorized access, fraud, or technical threats.
  • Maintain appropriate records of communications and decisions.
  • Comply with legal obligations, enforce applicable terms, or protect legitimate rights and interests.

Information submitted through the general inquiry form is not intended to be used for automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

Legal Bases for Processing

Where data protection law requires a legal basis, processing may rely on one or more of the following:

Legal basis Possible context Examples
Consent Where you make an optional choice or provide permission. Non-essential cookies, optional communications, or another clearly consent-based feature.
Steps at your request Where you ask for information before a possible arrangement. Course, workshop, training, advertising, or collaboration inquiries.
Legitimate interests Where processing is reasonably necessary and does not override your rights. Responding to inquiries, maintaining records, website security, preventing abuse, and improving operations.
Legal obligation Where information must be processed to comply with applicable law. Responding to valid legal requests, maintaining required records, or meeting regulatory duties.
Legal claims and protection Where necessary to establish, exercise, or defend rights. Disputes, misuse investigations, enforcement, fraud prevention, or security incidents.

The applicable basis depends on the specific processing activity, the information involved, and the relevant jurisdiction.

Cookies and Similar Technologies

The website may use cookies, local storage, session storage, server logs, pixels, or similar technologies for essential operation, security, preference storage, performance measurement, and analytics where enabled.

Possible categories

  • Essential technologies: support security, navigation, form operation, consent storage, and core website functions.
  • Preference technologies: remember settings such as consent selections or interface choices.
  • Analytics technologies: help understand general website use and performance where permitted.
  • Advertising technologies: may be used only where implemented, permitted, and appropriately disclosed.

You can review more detailed information in the Cookie Policy. Browser controls may allow you to block, delete, or limit cookies, although some website features may not work as expected afterward.

When Information May Be Disclosed

Information is not sold as a standalone product. It may be disclosed in limited circumstances, including:

  • To hosting, email, security, analytics, technical support, form processing, storage, or other service providers that support website operations.
  • To professional advisers where legal, accounting, security, insurance, compliance, or other specialist support is reasonably necessary.
  • To a relevant provider, educator, trainer, advertiser, collaborator, or other organization only where appropriate, permitted, and consistent with the inquiry context.
  • To authorities, courts, regulators, law enforcement, or other parties where required or permitted by law.
  • To protect users, the website, Rolewise AI, service providers, or others against abuse, fraud, security threats, legal claims, or harmful conduct.
  • In connection with a merger, reorganization, financing, sale, transfer, or similar business transaction, subject to appropriate safeguards.
Provider and collaboration inquiries

Information will not automatically be forwarded to an external provider merely because an inquiry is submitted. Any possible introduction or disclosure depends on relevance, suitability, permission, available processes, and the circumstances of the inquiry.

International Data Transfers

Website hosting, email, analytics, security, or other service providers may process information in countries different from your own. Privacy laws and government access rules may differ between jurisdictions.

Where required, reasonable transfer mechanisms and safeguards may be used, such as adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, contractual protections, provider assessments, technical controls, or other legally recognized measures.

No transfer method or security measure can eliminate every risk. You should avoid submitting information that is unnecessary or particularly sensitive.

Data Retention

Information may be retained only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including inquiry management, communication, recordkeeping, security, dispute handling, legal compliance, and protection of legitimate interests.

Retention periods may depend on:

  • The type and sensitivity of the information.
  • The nature and status of the inquiry.
  • Whether communication remains active or relevant.
  • Legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, insurance, or contractual requirements.
  • Security, fraud prevention, dispute, and enforcement needs.
  • Technical backup and deletion cycles.

Information may be deleted, anonymized, aggregated, or retained in restricted form when it is no longer needed for active use.

Information Security

Reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures may be used to reduce the risk of unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, loss, misuse, or destruction.

Measures may include:

  • Transport encryption where supported.
  • Access restrictions and account controls.
  • Security updates, monitoring, and logging.
  • Spam, abuse, and automated-submission controls.
  • Limited access by service providers and authorized personnel.
  • Backup, recovery, and incident-response procedures.

No website, network, storage platform, email system, or transmission method is completely secure. You submit information at your own risk and should limit submissions to what is necessary.

Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location and the circumstances, you may have rights regarding personal information, including the right to:

  • Request access to personal information.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Request deletion in certain circumstances.
  • Request restriction of processing.
  • Object to certain processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent where processing relies on consent.
  • Request data portability where applicable.
  • Complain to an appropriate supervisory authority.

Rights are not absolute. A request may be limited or refused where an exemption applies, identity cannot be reasonably verified, information must be retained by law, the request would adversely affect another person, or another lawful reason applies.
