Sales Context

AI Through the Sales Work You Already Do

Sales work combines research, relationship building, discovery, communication, documentation, commercial judgment, coordination, and responsible follow-through.

AI may support account research organization, meeting preparation, question development, follow-up drafts, CRM note structure, proposal outlines, pipeline summaries, and coaching preparation when the request includes verified information and clear boundaries.

Final sales work still requires people to verify account facts, customer needs, pricing, product capabilities, commitments, legal or contractual language, permissions, privacy, CRM accuracy, commercial approval, and the suitability of every external communication.

  • Begin with the account, customer objective, stage, known context, and intended next step.
  • Use approved product information, value propositions, pricing, claims, and commercial boundaries.
  • Define review ownership for communication, CRM records, proposals, commitments, and escalation.

Prompts With Customer Context

Adapt every example to verified account information, customer permission, approved product claims, pricing rules, commercial boundaries, CRM policy, and your review process.

Meeting preparation

Prepare for a Customer Meeting

Using only the verified account and opportunity information below, organize a meeting preparation brief with these sections: meeting objective, known customer context, confirmed stakeholders, prior activity, approved value proposition, relevant product information, discovery questions, assumptions requiring validation, risks, prohibited claims, internal owners, and desired next steps. Do not invent account facts, customer needs, budgets, timelines, product capabilities, commercial terms, or likely outcomes.

Human review required Adapt before use
Customer follow-up

Draft a Customer Follow-Up

Using only the approved meeting notes below, prepare a concise customer follow-up draft that includes confirmed discussion points, agreed actions, responsible parties, approved materials, known timelines, unresolved questions, and the proposed next step. Do not add pricing, discounts, product capabilities, commitments, legal language, implementation promises, deadlines, or outcomes that are not explicitly present in the approved notes.

Human review required Adapt before use
CRM documentation

Structure a CRM Update

Using only the authorized sales notes below, prepare a structured CRM update with these fields: activity summary, confirmed customer priorities, stakeholders, opportunity stage evidence, objections, risks, dependencies, approved commitments, next actions, responsible owners, target dates already confirmed, information gaps, and items requiring manager review. Do not infer intent, sentiment, probability, forecast category, budget, authority, timeline, or purchase likelihood.

Human review required Adapt before use
