What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files that a website or service provider may place on your browser or device. They can store identifiers, preferences, consent choices, security information, or other limited data.

Cookies may be:

  • Session cookies: temporary files that generally expire when you close the browser.
  • Persistent cookies: files that remain for a defined period or until deleted.
  • First-party cookies: placed by the website you are visiting.
  • Third-party cookies: placed by an external service integrated with or accessed through the website.

Similar functions may also be performed through local storage, session storage, cache data, pixels, scripts, software development kits, server logs, or device identifiers.

Essential Technologies

Essential technologies support functions that are reasonably necessary for the website to operate securely and as expected.

They may be used to:

  • Remember whether you accepted, rejected, or customized optional cookie categories.
  • Support contact-form validation, spam prevention, and submission security.
  • Maintain website stability, navigation, and accessibility functions.
  • Prevent abuse, detect suspicious activity, and protect infrastructure.
  • Balance traffic, deliver pages, and maintain technical sessions.
  • Store temporary values needed for requested website features.
Essential does not mean unlimited

Technologies should be treated as essential only where they are reasonably necessary for a requested service, security, legal compliance, consent storage, or core website operation.

Preference Technologies

Preference technologies may remember selections that change how the website appears or behaves.

Examples may include:

  • Cookie consent selections.
  • Accessibility or reduced-motion preferences.
  • Language, region, or interface choices.
  • Dismissed notices or saved website settings.
  • Form or navigation preferences where implemented.

Blocking or deleting preference storage may cause the website to forget your selections and ask you to choose them again.

Analytics and Performance Technologies

Analytics technologies may help understand how the website is used, whether pages load correctly, where technical issues occur, and which content areas receive general engagement.

Possible information may include:

  • Pages viewed and approximate navigation paths.
  • General session duration and interaction events.
  • Browser, device, language, and operating-system information.
  • Approximate region derived from technical information.
  • Referring source, campaign parameters, and landing page.
  • Performance measurements, error events, and response times.

Where required, analytics technologies should remain inactive until you provide consent. Available consent settings may not prevent all server-level security or operational logging.

Analytics information may be aggregated, truncated, pseudonymized, or configured to reduce unnecessary collection where technically available.

Advertising and Campaign Technologies

Advertising or campaign technologies may be introduced to measure promotional activity, understand how visitors reach the website, limit repeated advertising, or evaluate campaign performance.

Where implemented, such technologies may process:

  • Campaign, referral, or attribution parameters.
  • Page visits and limited interaction events.
  • Browser or device identifiers.
  • Approximate location or audience information.
  • Conversion events such as reaching or submitting an inquiry form.

Advertising technologies should not be treated as essential merely because they support marketing. Where applicable law requires consent, they should remain optional until consent is provided.

Third-Party Technologies

External services may set or access cookies and similar technologies when their scripts, fonts, media, security tools, analytics services, forms, links, or other components are used.

Possible categories of external providers include:

  • Website hosting and content-delivery providers.
  • Security, spam prevention, firewall, and abuse-detection providers.
  • Analytics and performance-monitoring providers.
  • Font, icon, media, video, map, or content-embedding providers.
  • Email delivery, form processing, and communication providers.
  • Advertising, campaign, or affiliate measurement providers where implemented.

Third parties may process information under their own terms and privacy policies. Rolewise AI does not control every external technology after it is loaded or when you leave the website.

Review the relevant provider's policies and browser controls before enabling optional integrations or interacting with external content.

Consent and Cookie Preferences

Where a consent mechanism is available, you may be able to accept all optional categories, reject optional categories, or customize your preferences.

Your choice may be saved in browser storage so the website can remember it. If you:

  • Delete browser storage, the website may ask for your choice again.
  • Use another browser or device, you may need to set preferences again.
  • Use private-browsing mode, preferences may expire when the session ends.
  • Block storage completely, the consent tool may not remember your selection.

You may change available preferences through the cookie-settings control where implemented.

Changing preferences generally affects future optional processing. It may not automatically delete information previously processed under a valid legal basis.

Browser and Device Controls

Most browsers allow you to inspect, block, restrict, or delete cookies and website storage. Available steps depend on the browser and device.

You may be able to:

  • Block all cookies.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Delete stored cookies and local-storage values.
  • Clear browsing data when the browser closes.
  • Restrict cross-site tracking.
  • Use private or incognito browsing.
  • Disable JavaScript or external content.
  • Install privacy, content-blocking, or tracking-protection extensions.

Blocking essential storage, JavaScript, fonts, content-delivery services, or security tools may affect navigation, forms, icons, consent controls, performance, or other website features.

Storage Duration

Cookies and similar technologies may remain for different periods depending on their purpose and configuration.

Session duration Temporary values may expire when a browser tab, session, or browser is closed.
Short-term storage Security, load-balancing, error-detection, or temporary preference values may remain for minutes, hours, or days.
Preference storage Consent and interface choices may remain for several months or until deleted, renewed, or replaced.
Analytics storage Optional analytics identifiers or event information may remain for a provider-defined period, subject to configuration and applicable law.

Storage periods may be changed to reflect legal requirements, technical needs, provider settings, security considerations, or data-minimization practices.

Do Not Track and Similar Signals

Some browsers or devices send Do Not Track, Global Privacy Control, or similar preference signals.

Because technical standards and legal requirements differ, the website's response may depend on the available systems, the signal received, the technology involved, and the applicable jurisdiction.

Where legally required and technically supported, recognized privacy preference signals may be treated as a request to limit certain optional processing.

Browser signals do not necessarily disable essential security, server logging, fraud prevention, consent storage, or other processing required to provide and protect the website.
